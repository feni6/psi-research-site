# Path 3: "I'm new -- give me the lay of the land"


*A chronological greatest-hits tour from the 1930s to the 2020s. One paper per era, covering the major domains and debates as they unfolded historically.*

1. **Gurney, Myers & Podmore (1886)** -- *Phantasms of the Living*
   `gurney_myers_podmore_1886_phantasms` | The founding text of systematic psychical research. Combines 702 verified crisis-apparition cases with controlled thought-transference experiments (17,653 card trials, p < 0.00000002) to build the first empirical case for telepathy. Introduces the statistical logic of comparing observed anomalous coincidence rates against chance — the framework every subsequent meta-analysis in this library inherits.

2. **Rhine (1934)** -- *Extra-Sensory Perception*
   `rhine_1934_extrasensory_perception` | The foundational monograph that launched modern experimental parapsychology at Duke University. Introduces the term "ESP," Zener card methodology, and statistical approach. Reports 64,000+ trials with X values reaching 75.3. Sets the paradigm for all subsequent research.

3. **Duane & Behrendt (1965)** -- *Extrasensory Electroencephalographic Induction Between Identical Twins*
   `duane_1965_extrasensory` | The earliest entry in the library, published in *Science*. Sets the stage: can separated minds show correlated brain activity?

3. **Targ & Puthoff (1974)** -- *Information Transmission Under Conditions of Sensory Shielding*
   `targ_1974_information` | The SRI remote viewing paper in *Nature*. Launches a government-funded program and a decades-long debate.

4. **Jahn (1982)** -- *The Persistent Paradox of Psychic Phenomena: An Engineering Perspective*
   `jahn_1982_persistent` | A Princeton engineering dean takes psychokinesis seriously in the *Proceedings of the IEEE*. Founds the PEAR laboratory.

5. **Schmidt (1987)** -- *The Strange Properties of Psychokinesis*
   `schmidt_1987_strange` | Helmut Schmidt's influential overview of PK research, including retroactive PK on pre-recorded random events.

6. **Rao & Palmer (1987)** -- *The Anomaly Called Psi: Recent Research and Criticism*
   `rao_1987_anomaly` | A balanced review of the state of the field as of the late 1980s, covering both positive findings and methodological critiques.

7. **Honorton & Ferrari (1989)** -- *Future Telling: A Meta-Analysis of Forced-Choice Precognition Experiments, 1935--1987*
   `honorton_1989_future` | Fifty years of forced-choice precognition data, meta-analyzed. The cumulative odds against chance are staggering.

8. **Radin & Nelson (1989)** -- *Evidence for Consciousness-Related Anomalies in Random Physical Systems*
   `radin_1989_evidence` | The landmark micro-PK meta-analysis. Establishes that the effect is tiny but extraordinarily consistent across hundreds of studies.

9. **Josephson & Pallikari-Viras (1991)** -- *Biological Utilisation of Quantum Nonlocality*
   `josephson_1991_biological` | A Nobel laureate (Josephson) proposes that quantum nonlocality could underlie psi. A rare high-prestige theoretical framework.

10. **Bem & Honorton (1994)** -- *Does Psi Exist? Replicable Evidence for an Anomalous Process of Information Transfer*
   `bem_1994_does` | The autoganzfeld paper in *Psychological Bulletin*. Designed to answer every criticism Hyman raised. A turning point in the debate.

11. **Hyman (1994)** -- *Anomaly or Artifact? Comments on Bem and Honorton*
    `hyman_1994_anomaly` | The immediate skeptical response. Read it back-to-back with the paper above to see the debate at its sharpest.

12. **Utts (1996)** -- *An Assessment of the Evidence for Psychic Functioning*
    `utts_1996_assessment` | The statistician's report for the CIA Stargate review. Concludes remote viewing works but stops short of endorsing the mechanism.

13. **Milton & Wiseman (1999)** -- *Does Psi Exist? Lack of Replication*
    `milton_1999_does` | The replication crisis hits ganzfeld. A meta-analysis of newer studies finds no significant effect.

12b. **Bem, Palmer & Broughton (2001)** -- *Updating the Ganzfeld Meta-Analysis: A Response to Milton and Wiseman*
    `bem_palmer_broughton_2001_updating_ganzfeld` | Direct response to Milton & Wiseman showing the ganzfeld effect persists (31.2% hit rate, Z=3.49, p=.0002) when protocol adherence is maintained. Demonstrates that study quality, not year, predicts outcomes.

14. **van Lommel et al. (2001)** -- *Near-Death Experience in Survivors of Cardiac Arrest*
    `lommel_2001_neardeath` | Published in *The Lancet*. A prospective study of NDEs during cardiac arrest, opening a new empirical front.

15. **Nelson et al. (2002)** -- *Correlations of Continuous Random Data with Major World Events*
    `nelson_2002_correlations` | The Global Consciousness Project: do RNG outputs worldwide correlate with events like 9/11? Ambitious and controversial.

16. **Parker & Brusewitz (2003)** -- *A Compendium of the Evidence for Psi*
    `parker_2003_compendium` | A 2003 annotated review covering all major paradigms from classical studies through then-current meta-analyses. A natural survey point between the GCP launch (2002) and the Bem era (2011). Effect sizes across domains: free-response d=0.16, DMILS d=0.11, dream-ESP d=0.33, extraversion d=0.20. Provides historical depth before you enter the modern statistical debates.

17. **Sheldrake, Morgana & Sheldrake (2003)** -- *Testing a Language-Using Parrot for Telepathy*
    `sheldrake_2003_parrot_telepathy` | Same year as Parker's compendium — a single-experiment study that illustrates what a controlled behavioral psi claim looks like at its best. The African Grey parrot N'kisi names what his owner (in a separate room) is viewing in sealed photographs; 131 trials, 23 hits vs. 12.2 expected, three blind transcribers, independent statistician. Stands in contrast to the statistical landscape of meta-analyses: one animal, one ability, clearly stated success criterion.

18. **Standish et al. (2004)** -- *Electroencephalographic Evidence of Correlated Event-Related Signals Between the Brains of Spatially and Sensory Isolated Human Subjects*
    `standish_2004_electroencephalographic` | The largest EEG correlation study (N=60) demonstrating that 8.3% of emotionally bonded pairs show significant brain-to-brain correlations (Stouffer z=-3.28, p=0.0005) when one member receives visual stimulation. Most methodologically rigorous demonstration from the Bastyr/UW lab, with seven independent laboratories reporting similar findings since 1963.

19. **Bem (2011)** -- *Feeling the Future: Experimental Evidence for Anomalous Retroactive Influences*
    `bem_2011_feeling` | Published in a top psychology journal, this paper on precognition triggers a firestorm about replication and statistical methods.

20. **Mossbridge, Tressoldi & Utts (2012)** -- *Predictive Physiological Anticipation Preceding Seemingly Unpredictable Stimuli*
    `mossbridge_2012_predictive` | Presentiment goes meta-analytic. The body seems to know what is coming before the stimulus is selected.

21. **Parnia et al. (2014)** -- *AWARE -- AWAreness during REsuscitation*
    `parnia_2014_aware` | The largest prospective study of consciousness during cardiac arrest. Rigorous hospital-based protocol with visual targets.

22. **Open Science Collaboration (2015)** -- *Estimating the Reproducibility of Psychological Science*
    `open_science_2015_reproducibility` | The broader replication crisis. Context for understanding why psi's replication struggles are not unique.

23. **Cardena (2018)** -- *The Experimental Evidence for Parapsychological Phenomena: A Review*
    `cardeña_2018_experimental` | The current state of play, in *American Psychologist*. A comprehensive, peer-reviewed summary of where things stand.

24. **Radin (2023)** -- *Sentiment and Presentiment in Twitter: Do Trends in Collective Mood "Feel the Future"?*
    `radin_2023_sentiment` | An accessible example of how psi research has expanded to Big Data: 13 years of Twitter sentiment in 10 languages shows a statistically significant anticipatory decline before unpredictable negative world events. Bridges individual presentiment psychophysiology with GCP-style collective-consciousness research, and illustrates how a publicly verifiable dataset can serve as an independent test.

25. **Radin (2025)** -- *Observer Influence on Quantum Interference: Testing the von Neumann-Wigner Consciousness-Collapse Theory*
    `radin_2025_observer` | The latest double-slit consciousness experiment. Shows the research program is ongoing and evolving.
