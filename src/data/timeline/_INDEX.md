## Year-to-Era Mapping

Use this table to quickly determine which era a paper belongs to:

| Year Range | Era | File | Key Theme |
|------------|-----|------|-----------|

| 2016–present | 6 (Current) | `era_6_current.md` | Open science; replication crisis; registered reports |

**For papers from 1970-1999**, use psi_domain to decide:
- Remote viewing → Era 4 (Stargate)
- Ganzfeld, telepathy, PK, precognition, methodology → Era 3 (Ganzfeld)
- If both apply, check both era files

## AI Agent Instructions: Knowledge-Structure Update

When processing a new paper, determine whether it belongs in the timeline:

1. **Default: Don't add.** The timeline is narrative history, not an exhaustive bibliography. Most papers are NOT historically significant enough.
2. **Only proceed if the paper:**
   - Represents a historical turning point, paradigm shift, or landmark result
   - Describes a new methodology that becomes widely adopted
   - Is cited in 10+ subsequent papers or major meta-analyses
   - Addresses a major historical controversy
3. **DO NOT read full era files.** Use the Year-to-Era Mapping above to identify the era, then Grep the relevant era file for the paper's author/title before opening.
4. **If adding:** Place in the `📄 In library:` section under the appropriate subsection heading. Maintain chronological order.
